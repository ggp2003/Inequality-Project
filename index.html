01

Start with the full historical frame

The comparable series begins in 1915. Both countries enter the twentieth-century record with high pretax inequality, but the century that follows does not move them in lockstep.

02

France compresses sharply

The French line falls through the upheaval around World War II and remains lower through much of the postwar period.

03

The US line bends upward

The United States follows a different late-century path. Inequality begins rising more visibly from the late 1970s and 1980s.

04

The comparison becomes the story

Once both lines are visible, the main pattern is the widening distance between them. The US increasingly sits above France in the pretax Gini series.

05

Make the gap visible

The purple line tracks the US-France difference directly. Values above zero mean the US Gini is higher than France's in that year.

06

A persistent divide

By the end of the series, the countries remain separated. This first scrollytelling pass turns the raw comparison into a narrative spine for the broader inequality project.

Data note

This prototype uses the project file 2. Data/gini_coefficient.csv. The web-ready data aligns US and France observations by year and computes the difference as United States minus France.
